# Corpus Analysis of NMCOSS

## Overview
An exploratory corpus analysis, using Python, of the New Mexico and Colorado Spanish Survey (NMCOSS) for sociolinguistic research, focusing on terms for family members in English.

## Research Questions

- What is the frequency and diffusion of the varying synonyms of English and Spanish family terms in the New Mexico Spanish? 
  -grandmother, grandfather, mother, father
  
- How incorporated and mixed are the English and Spanish terms in the language of individual speakers?

## Methods 

- Corpus Linguistics and textmining
- looked at word frequency and diffusion
- utilized unigrams, bigrams, and trigrams to collect the context around the English borrowings in Spanish

## Findings
- Corpus-level:
    - English synonymic family terms do appear in NMCOSS
    - In mixed contexts
    - Overall low levels of diffusion for English terms
    - Frequency of terms can also be dependent on topic:
    - Frequency of “escuela” > 200
    - Presence of code-switching and English in corpus

- Individual level
      - Few speakers use both English and Spanish terms variably

- Spanish terms still mostly preferred

- “mamá” and “papá” with the highest frequency and diffusion

- “grandpa”, “grandma”, “dad” with highest diffusion of English terms
